The public version of the report is based on documents, payroll and settlement data, correspondence, and a timeline. Its purpose is to allow institutions to quickly understand the core of the case and identify material requiring further, careful review. The descriptions are legally cautious: they concern material for further independent assessment and do not constitute final court findings. Last updated: 2026-04-21 · Public version: briefing / anonymized ## What this concerns The case primarily concerns wage settlements, deductions, work planning, and subsequent actions taken before institutions and in relation to legal representatives. The public documentation indicates that the central issues are discrepancies concerning guaranteed working hours, settlements under Phase C / 32h, disputed debt calculations, the content of communication with the worker, and the consistency of data shown in payslips. ## Main reference points 1. The court stage of 12.03.2024, publicly described as the proces-verbaal, and the key reference point for further calculations and document comparisons. 2. The lack of full documentary closure in the period 04–05.2024 and further actions involving another legal representative in 06–07.2024. 3. Institutional escalation in 2025–2026, including complaints, further correspondence, and the independent retrieval of some documents. 4. The court material from 12.03.2024, loonspecificaties, and data concerning the 32h / Phase C guarantee. 5. Correspondence concerning debt and settlements, recordings from 13.11.2023 and 30.01.2024, and selected examples of documentary discrepancies in payslips. ## Main conclusion The main conclusion arising from the public layer of the report is that the case requires an independent and transparent documentary assessment. The report indicates the need for a careful review of the documents, an institutional response where required, and an assessment based on documentation rather than on the parties’ statements alone. ## I request 1. a careful analysis of the submitted documentation, 2. an assessment of the consistency of settlements, deductions, payroll documentation, and correspondence, 3. an assessment of whether further institutional intervention is required in this case, 4. an indication of the further appropriate procedural path if the institution in question is not competent to examine the case in full. ## Access to materials The public layer of the report is anonymized and legally cautious. More complete supporting materials may be made available to an institution in a secure manner, upon justified contact and with due regard for data protection.
