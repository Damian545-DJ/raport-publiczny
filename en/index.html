In 30 seconds: what this case is about

The case concerns a migrant worker who had documents, payslips, correspondence and formal help routes, but still did not obtain a full, clear and verifiable review of payroll settlements, deductions, guaranteed working hours and the practical execution of post-court-stage arrangements.

Purpose of this page: to present anonymized material for independent verification — without publishing full personal data and without prejudging legal liability.

What requires independent verification

  • whether the 32-hour guarantee was respected in practice,
  • whether payslips are complete and consistent with documents and bank transfers,
  • whether deductions for accommodation, insurance and other items were correctly calculated,
  • whether the gross-to-net path was properly explained,
  • whether the process-verbaal from the court stage was fully executed,
  • whether institutions and legal representatives actually verified the key documents rather than only closing the matter formally.

Formal rights are not enough

This report is not only about a private employment dispute with one temporary work agency. The collected materials raise a broader question: whether the formal rights available to a migrant worker were effective in practice.

The worker used available channels: documents, payroll records, correspondence, institutional complaints, legal assistance and court-related procedures. Despite this, key issues remained insufficiently clarified for a long time, including guaranteed working hours, payslips, deductions, balances, settlement calculations and the practical enforcement of legal protection.

The central conclusion of this public report is simple: formal rights are not enough. Protection must be effective, transparent and enforceable in practice.
