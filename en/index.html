In 30 seconds: what this case is about

The case concerns migrant workers who had documents, payslips, correspondence and formal help routes, but still did not obtain a full, clear and verifiable review of payroll settlements, deductions and guaranteed working hours.

Purpose of this page: to present anonymized material for independent verification, without publishing full personal data and without prejudging responsibility.

What requires independent verification

  • whether the 32-hour guarantee was respected in practice,
  • whether payslips are complete and consistent with documents and bank transfers,
  • whether deductions for accommodation, insurance and other items were correctly calculated,
  • whether the gross-to-net path was properly explained,
  • whether the available materials were actually checked in a clear and verifiable way.

Case structure

  1. Documents: contract, payslips, bank transfers and correspondence.
  2. Discrepancies: 32 hours, deductions, missing weeks, gross-to-net path and balances.
  3. Reports and letters: institutions, representatives, requests and replies.
  4. No full clarification: no single complete and verifiable answer to the core issues.
  5. Conclusion: independent verification of the material is needed.

How to read this material

  1. Start with the core documents.
  2. Then check the discrepancies.
  3. Then review the correspondence.
  4. Finally assess whether protection was effective in practice.

Formal rights are not enough

This report is not only about a private employment dispute with one temporary work agency. The collected materials raise a broader question: whether the formal rights available to migrant workers were effective in practice.

The workers used available channels: documents, payroll records, correspondence, institutional contacts and legal assistance. Despite this, key issues remained insufficiently clarified for a long time.

The central conclusion is simple: formal rights are not enough. Protection must be effective, transparent and enforceable in practice.
