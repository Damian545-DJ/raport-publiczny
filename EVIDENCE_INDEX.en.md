---

## Publication-status legend

- **PUBLIC** — an anonymized description, table or excerpt is publicly available.
- **PUBLIC AFTER REDACTION** — the material may be published only after permanent removal of personal and identifying data.
- **PRIVATE — PUBLIC DESCRIPTION** — the original is held privately; only a safe description, date or result is public.
- **PRIVATE** — the material is not publicly shared; it may be supplied to a competent institution or verified legal representative.

The former labels **FULL-ONLY**, **SEMI (REDACTED)** and **PUBLIC (REDACTED)** are no longer used. They corresponded respectively to private, public after redaction, and public after redaction.

### SHA-256

A SHA-256 value may show that a specific file has not changed since the hash was calculated. It does not by itself establish that the file's contents are true. Where an item covers multiple files, each file should have a separate value in the private integrity register. This public index does not contain unverified or invented hashes.

## Private integrity register — required fields

For every original file, the non-public register should record:

1. the evidence ID from this index,
2. the exact safe filename,
3. acquisition date and source,
4. file size,
5. SHA-256 value,
6. whether the file is an original, export copy, translation or redacted version,
7. the hash-calculation date and the person who performed the check.

## Final rule

The index describes what material **may support**. It should not state that an item “proves” a matter until the material has been reviewed in full context, authenticated and compared with contrary evidence.
