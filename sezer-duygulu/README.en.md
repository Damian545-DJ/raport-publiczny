## Purpose of this section
This section was prepared as a supplement to the main public evidence report. Its purpose is to connect:
- publicly available information concerning Sezer Duygulu and entities linked to him,
- public red flags related to companies operating within this ecosystem,
- recent documented materials indicating inconsistencies in an individual case connected to Intrixo(Voorneputten).

## Public context
In publicly available materials, Sezer Duygulu is presented as a person linked to the Home of People group and to brands and entities operating in the temporary work and migrant labour sector, including, among others, Uitzendbureau Solutions, Intrixo(Voorneputten), Efficient at Work, T&S Flexwerk, NWH Jobs, and Masterteam.

The mere fact of being publicly linked to an extensive business ecosystem does not in itself prove responsibility for any specific violation. However, it is relevant context, especially where some of these entities have for years been associated with warning signs concerning labour rights, housing conditions, worker dependency on agencies, deductions, and lack of transparency in dealings with migrant workers.

## Public red flags
In the public domain, important warning signals have appeared concerning companies from this ecosystem. These have included, among other things:
- labour-law disputes,
- issues relating to the protection of agency workers,
- controversies regarding the living and working conditions of migrant workers,
- the need for increased oversight of housing-and-work arrangements.

These warning signals matter not because they automatically establish the liability of any individual, but because they form important context for assessing credibility, risk, and patterns in individual labour-related cases.

## Recent evidence and documented inconsistencies
In my individual case connected to Intrixo(Voorneputten), I have gathered recent materials indicating significant inconsistencies between:
- the content of correspondence,
- arrangements concerning leave,
- planning records,
- payroll/accounting records,
- and the version of events later presented to SNCU.

The documentation indicates that a period later described in a way that undermined prior leave arrangements was at the same time recorded in the planning system and in payroll-related documents as leave or as a period settled through leave days. This creates a clear documentary inconsistency.

## Why this matters
Taken separately, public red flags may be treated as background context, and an individual dispute may be seen as an isolated case. However, when viewed together, they suggest something more serious: a possible problem with the credibility of the explanations presented, the consistency of documentation, and the transparency of actions taken toward the worker.

That is why this material has been separated as a distinct section of the report. The aim is not to make emotional accusations, but to show that there are publicly visible warning signs and concrete, recent documentary inconsistencies that cannot be honestly ignored.

## Conclusion
At this stage, publicly available information and the evidence I have gathered do not by themselves constitute a final legal finding of personal liability on the part of Sezer Duygulu. They do, however, provide sufficient grounds to treat the activities of entities linked to this ecosystem as requiring increased attention, caution, and further scrutiny in relation to:
- labour rights,
- documentary transparency,
- housing conditions,
- the worker’s economic dependence on the agency,
- and the credibility of explanations submitted to institutions.
